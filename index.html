About Me

Who am I?

Hi I'm Will Carter. I am a current undergraduate student at Tufts University working to forge connections to the natural world through media, scientific practice, and storytelling. My specialty is in Science Communication, creating my own interdisciplinary major in the subject by combining coursework in Marine Biology, Media Practice, and Anthropology. I am passionate about increasing accessibility to education, frequently contributing to ongoing research and industry initiatives centered around utilizing audience behavior and fan studies to increase youth engagement in STEM. My more recent projects have touched on human-marine interaction, XR technology, marketing, communications, media production, and graphic design.

Outside of work, I spend the majority of my time outdoors. I am always exploring and am often either backpacking, swimming, surfing, hiking, running, or just soaking up the sun.

Experience

Work Experience

Coordinator: Experiences, Content and Curriculum, Meridian Treehouse 2021-Present

Produced communications campaigns, live events, short-form videos, Immersive exhibits, multimodal educational activities, graphics, and research projects. Meridian Treehouse is an award-winning nonfiction storytelling and experiential agency that cultivates multidisciplinary collaboration and innovation. The firm specializes in developing multi-platform experiences, content, and partnerships that educate and entertain with the goal of fostering curiosity, free from spatial and logistical constraints.

Exploration Technology Lab, National Geographic 2022-2022

Aided the Exploration Technology Lab at National Geographic via engineering hardware testing and design, management of scientist-led research expeditions, and active engagement with National Geographic Explorers, engineers, storytellers, and educators. Advanced conservation efforts through the support of the development of animal-borne data collection and imaging systems for both sperm whales and the deep sea.

Intern, Project CETI 2022-2022

Created storytelling, communications, mapping, technical schematics, and branding materials, assisted in curriculum architecture and design for a capacity building & conservation education program, working with engineers, advocates, educators, and storytelling and communications teams to support the mission of Project CETI. Project CETI brings together an interdisciplinary team of accomplished scientists and technologists to study the communication of sperm whales. Project CETI endeavors to provide the first-ever blueprint of another animal’s language, and over the next five years, will seek to understand sperm whales on a level never achieved previously.

Communications Advisor, The Hydrous 2020-2021

Designed and implemented digital communications strategies, honed abilities in immersive technology through augmented reality experiences, and synchronous virtual reality events, and taught marine biology classes using photogrammetry and XR media. The Hydrous is a 501(c)3 non-profit organization on a mission to inspire ocean empathy and marine stewardship. Through science-based experiences, scalable solutions like virtual reality, and multi-disciplinary partnerships, they make the ocean accessible so that all people may explore, understand, and engage with it.

Communications Manager, Sharks Pacific 2020-2021

Executed business and digital marketing strategies, designing brand campaigns, nonprofit style guides, short-form videos, AR content, and graphics for social media. Sharks Pacific works to communicate the importance of sharks in Pacific communities and to advocate for the conservation and responsible management of sharks and the related marine environment through research, outreach, and advocacy at the community, national and international levels.

Portfolio

Recent Work

The Treehouse Podcast

The Treehouse podcasts follow host Wesley Della Volla as he interviews some of the world's most prominent scientists, talking about their journies and standout moments during their careers. I aided in the production, development, and design of this podcast, including writing communications materials and graphic design work.

Dominica Marine Conservation Fellowship

In September 2022, Project CETI launched the Dominica Marine Conservation Fellowship, (DMCF) a community-based program building the next generation of local conservation leaders who will be equipped and empowered to protect Dominica’s marine environment and resources. I helped design the curriculum architecture, lesson plans, and education materials for DMCF, teaching workshops on conservation technology, GIS, and remote sensing.

North Atlantic Right Whale Storymap

The North Atlantic Right Whale is one of most endangered large whale species in the world due to the impacts humans pose on the ocean. I created a story map detailing the risks humans pose to these whales along the NorthEast coastline, highlighting abilities in database management, detailing the procedure of mapping in ArcGIS, and explaining the implications of this data.

An Introduction to Learning in the Metaverse

Advised An Introduction to Learning with the Metaverse in April 2022, collaborating with research teams, Meridian Treehouse, The Hydrous, and Meta Immersive Learning. This research guide shows the potential of the metaverse to enhance educational experiences, communicating research and case studies on XR (extended reality) and their implications in the classroom.

The Hydrous Style Guide

I created and implemented this style guide for The Hydrous, an ocean literacy nonprofit that combines science, design, and emerging technologies to create access to ocean exploration. I wrote communications plans and the brand story, creating all graphics.

The Global Biodiversity Festival

Created graphics and communications content, collaborating with Exploring by the Seat of your pants around The Global Biodiversity Festival, a virtual weekend for the general public with the goal of shining a spotlight on biodiversity loss. Raising $30,000 for conservation, the festival broadcasted live for over 80 hours, featuring over 160 speakers from over 7 continents and 60+ countries.
